What is cupping?

The simplest way to really notice your coffee

Most of us drink coffee on autopilot. Cupping asks you to slow down for fifteen minutes and actually pay attention — to the smell before the water goes in, the aroma as it steeps, the way the flavour changes as the cup cools.

It's the standard method used by roasters, importers and baristas to evaluate coffee. But there's no reason it has to stay in the warehouse. Brewed in a bowl. Slurped off a spoon. That's really all it is.

"If it tastes like chocolate to you, it tastes like chocolate. There are no wrong answers."

You don't need experience. You don't need expensive equipment. You need curiosity — and we'll walk you through the rest.

How it works

Simple from start to finish

The whole session takes about twenty minutes. Here's what to expect.

01

Name your cupping

Give the session a name — anything you like. The coffee, the morning, a feeling. It's just for you.

02

Grind and smell

Start with a medium-coarse grind and take in the dry fragrance before a drop of water touches it.

03

Pour and wait

Pour just-boiled water, start the built-in timer, and let the crust form. Don't stir. Just smell.

04

Break the crust

Push the floating grounds aside and inhale. This moment — the wet aroma — is one of the best parts of cupping.

05

Slurp and taste

Scoop a spoonful and slurp it loudly. Yes, really. It coats your whole palate and unlocks flavours you'd otherwise miss.

06

Pick your flavours

Use the built-in flavour wheel to put words to what you tasted. Up to four picks — as broad or specific as you like.
