What is cupping?

Tasting coffee the simple way

Cupping is just a simple, consistent way to taste and think about coffee. You brew it directly in a cup — no filters, no machines — and slurp it off a spoon. That's really it.

Coffee professionals use it to compare batches side by side, but at home it's a lovely way to slow down and really notice what you're drinking — the smell, the flavour, the way it feels in your mouth.

🌟

There are no right answers. Everyone's palate is different. If it tastes like chocolate to you, it tastes like chocolate. Trust yourself.

Before you start

What you'll need

🫘

Fresh Coffee

Freshly ground is best. About 10–12g per cup.

🫙

A Cup or Bowl

Any mug or bowl works. A cereal bowl is perfect.

🫖

Hot Water

Just off the boil — around 90–95°C.

🥄

A Spoon

For breaking the crust and tasting.

⏱

A Timer

Your phone's timer works perfectly.

📝

Something to Write

To jot notes as you taste.

optional

⚖️

Aim for roughly 1 heaped tablespoon of grounds per cup of water. You don't need scales — close enough is fine.
